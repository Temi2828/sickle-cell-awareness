What is Sickle Cell Disease?

🧬

Genetic Condition

Sickle cell disease is an inherited blood disorder affecting red blood cells. It occurs when a person inherits the sickle cell gene from both parents.

🩸

How It Affects You

Abnormal hemoglobin causes red blood cells to become rigid and sickle-shaped, leading to pain, organ damage, and other serious complications.

🌍

Global Impact

Approximately 100,000 Americans have sickle cell disease, with millions more affected worldwide. Early detection saves lives.

❤️

Living with SCD

With proper medical care, education, and support, people with sickle cell disease can live fulfilling lives and manage their condition effectively.

Education & Awareness

🩺

Symptoms to Know

  • Severe pain in chest, back, or joints
  • Fatigue and shortness of breath
  • Swelling of hands and feet
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
  • Frequent infections
  • Delayed growth in children
💊

Treatment Options

  • Pain management and medication
  • Blood transfusions when needed
  • Hydroxycarbamide therapy
  • Stem cell and bone marrow transplants
  • Gene therapy (emerging treatments)
  • Comprehensive medical support
🤝

Living Well

  • Regular medical check-ups
  • Stay hydrated and maintain healthy diet
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
  • Manage stress and emotional health
  • Join support communities
  • Educate family and friends

Early Genotype Testing

Why Test?

Early detection allows for:

  • Early intervention and treatment
  • Better disease management
  • Prevention of complications
  • Informed family planning
  • Peace of mind

Who Should Test?

Consider testing if:

  • Family history of sickle cell disease
  • African or Caribbean descent
  • Hispanic or Mediterranean heritage
  • Middle Eastern or Asian ancestry
  • Planning to have children

Blood Donation Saves Lives

Why Blood Donation Matters

People with sickle cell disease often require regular blood transfusions. Your donation can:

  • ✓ Save lives of people with SCD
  • ✓ Help those needing surgery or in emergencies
  • ✓ Support cancer and trauma patients
  • ✓ Be life-changing for recipients

Donation Requirements

👤

Age: 16-65 years old

⚖️

Weight: At least 50 kg (110 lbs)

❤️

Reducing Stigma Through Accuracy

❌ Myth

"Sickle cell only affects Black people"

✓ Truth

While most common in African and Caribbean populations, SCD affects people of all ethnic backgrounds worldwide.

❌ Myth

"People with SCD can't work or have normal lives"

✓ Truth

Many people with SCD pursue careers, education, and build fulfilling lives with proper management and support.

❌ Myth

"You can catch sickle cell disease"

✓ Truth

SCD is purely genetic and inherited. It's not contagious and cannot be transmitted to others.

❌ Myth

"There's no hope for people with SCD"

✓ Truth

Medical advances, gene therapy, and stem cell transplants are opening new possibilities for treatment and cure.
